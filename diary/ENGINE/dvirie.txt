# Inspection Intelligence Engine (DVIRIE)

**ID:** `dvirie`
**Name:** Inspection Intelligence
**Description:** DVIR results and open defects

## Purpose
The DVIRIE engine processes and tracks Driver Vehicle Inspection Report (DVIR) results, specifically focusing on identifying and managing open defects. It stores and retrieves `dvirie_scores` in the database, helping to ensure vehicle safety and compliance.

## Configuration Details

The DVIRIE engine's operational parameters are defined as follows:

-   **Database Table:** `dvirie_scores`
-   **Score Field:** `score` (a numerical value between 0 and 100, possibly indicating inspection compliance or severity of defects)
-   **Required POST Fields:** `vehicle_id`, `client_id`, `score`
-   **Insertable Fields:**
    -   `vehicle_id`
    -   `client_id`
    -   `score`
    -   `last_inspection_at` (Timestamp of the last inspection)
    -   `open_defect_count`
    -   `is_road_safe` (Boolean: 0 or 1, indicating if the vehicle is safe for road operation)
    -   `computed_at` (Timestamp when the score was computed)
-   **Filterable GET Fields:**
    -   `client_id`
    -   `vehicle_id`

## Formulas and Logic

The DVIRIE engine's logic involves:

-   **Score Validation:** The `score` field must be a number between 0 and 100.
-   **Timestamping:** If `computed_at` is not provided in a POST request, it defaults to the current UTC timestamp.
-   **Boolean Field Handling:** `is_road_safe` is stored as an INTEGER (0 for false, 1 for true) in the SQLite database.
-   **Data Filtering:** GET requests allow filtering by `client_id` and `vehicle_id`.
-   **Ordering and Limiting:** GET results are ordered by `computed_at` DESC and limited to a maximum of 500 records.
-   **Authorization:**
    -   GET requests require `admin` or `dev` roles.
    -   POST requests require the `dev` role.
-   **Audit Logging:** Every successful POST operation logs an audit event.
