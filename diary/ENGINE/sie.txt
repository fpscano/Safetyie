# Safety Intelligence Engine (SIE)

**ID:** `sie`
**Name:** Safety Intelligence
**Description:** Master engine - final determination score

## Purpose
The SIE engine acts as a "master engine," consolidating and synthesizing scores from other intelligence engines to produce a final, comprehensive safety determination score. This score, along with a determination level, provides an overarching view of safety status. It stores and retrieves `sie_scores` in the database.

## Configuration Details

The SIE engine's operational parameters are defined as follows:

-   **Database Table:** `sie_scores`
-   **Score Field:** `determination_score` (a numerical value between 0 and 100, representing the overall safety score)
-   **Required POST Fields:** `vehicle_id`, `client_id`, `determination_score`, `determination_level`
-   **Insertable Fields:**
    -   `vehicle_id`
    -   `driver_id`
    -   `client_id`
    -   `determination_score`
    -   `determination_level` (e.g., "safe", "monitor", "intervene", "out_of_service")
    -   `hie_score` (Score from Hours of Service Intelligence Engine)
    -   `eie_score` (Score from Event Intelligence Engine)
    -   `fie_score` (Score from Fault Intelligence Engine)
    -   `tie_score` (Score from Telematics Intelligence Engine)
    -   `dvirie_score` (Score from Inspection Intelligence Engine)
    -   `wie_score` (Score from Weather Intelligence Engine)
    -   `pie_score` (Score from Prediction Intelligence Engine)
    -   `computed_at` (Timestamp when the score was computed)
-   **Filterable GET Fields:**
    -   `client_id`
    -   `vehicle_id`
    -   `driver_id`

## Formulas and Logic

The SIE engine's logic involves:

-   **Score Validation:** The `determination_score` field must be a number between 0 and 100.
-   **Determination Level Validation:** The `determination_level` must be one of "safe", "monitor", "intervene", or "out_of_service".
-   **Timestamping:** If `computed_at` is not provided in a POST request, it defaults to the current UTC timestamp.
-   **Data Filtering:** GET requests allow filtering by `client_id`, `vehicle_id`, and `driver_id`.
-   **Ordering and Limiting:** GET results are ordered by `computed_at` DESC and limited to a maximum of 500 records.
-   **Authorization:**
    -   GET requests require `admin` or `dev` roles.
    -   POST requests require the `dev` role.
-   **Audit Logging:** Every successful POST operation logs an audit event.
