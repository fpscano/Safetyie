# Weather Intelligence Engine (WIE)

**ID:** `wie`
**Name:** Weather Intelligence
**Description:** Weather conditions at vehicle locations

## Purpose
The WIE engine integrates and analyzes weather conditions relevant to vehicle locations. It stores and retrieves `wie_scores` in the database, providing insights into environmental factors affecting operations.

## Configuration Details

The WIE engine's operational parameters are defined as follows:

-   **Database Table:** `wie_scores`
-   **Score Field:** `score` (a numerical value between 0 and 100, potentially representing weather impact or severity)
-   **Required POST Fields:** `vehicle_id`, `client_id`, `score`
-   **Insertable Fields:**
    -   `vehicle_id`
    -   `client_id`
    -   `score`
    -   `weather_condition` (e.g., "sunny", "rain", "snow")
    -   `severity` (e.g., "low", "medium", "high")
    -   `advisory` (e.g., "fog_warning", "ice_alert")
    -   `computed_at` (Timestamp when the score was computed)
-   **Filterable GET Fields:**
    -   `client_id`
    -   `vehicle_id`

## Operational Mechanics (Data Flow)

The WIE engine processes data through GET and POST requests, similar to other engines.

## Formulas and Logic

The WIE engine's logic involves:

-   **Score Validation:** The `score` field must be a number between 0 and 100.
-   **Timestamping:** If `computed_at` is not provided in a POST request, it defaults to the current UTC timestamp.
-   **Data Filtering:** GET requests allow filtering by `client_id` and `vehicle_id`.
-   **Ordering and Limiting:** GET results are ordered by `computed_at` DESC and limited to a maximum of 500 records.
-   **Authorization:**
    -   GET requests require `admin` or `dev` roles.
    -   POST requests require the `dev` role.
-   **Audit Logging:** Every successful POST operation logs an audit event.
